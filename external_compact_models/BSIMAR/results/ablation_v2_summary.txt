Analysis:
---------
1. Exp 1 → Exp 2: Scheduled sampling HELPS. R2_norm improved from 0.9448 → 0.9665.
   gds NRMSE improved dramatically (190660% → 20.75%). gm improved (152% → 14.38%).
   This confirms scheduled sampling closes the train-test gap.

2. Exp 3 & 4 (with --reorder): CATASTROPHIC FAILURE. R2_norm went deeply negative.
   These were killed mid-training, so the reorder models may not have converged yet.
   However, the negative R2_norm suggests the reorder + unreorder pipeline may have a bug,
   OR the model needs much longer training with reordered outputs to learn the new sequence.

3. The --reorder flag changes the autoregressive order (charges first, id last).
   This fundamentally changes what the model learns and may need different hyperparameters.
   The killed-early results are NOT reliable for --reorder experiments.

Conclusion:
-----------
- Scheduled sampling (Exp 2) is the clear winner for improving AR accuracy.
- Output reordering needs further investigation (longer training or bug check).
- R2_norm is the correct metric; physical-space R2 is meaningless for log-transformed data.
- Best model: Exp 2 (SS only), avg R2_norm = 0.9665.
