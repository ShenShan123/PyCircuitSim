# v5 Experiment E3: Fine-tune BSIMAR NMOS on TSMC7 Only (2026-04-22)

## Hypothesis

TSMC7 is the worst-performing TSMC technology in the v4-shipping
universal BSIMAR NMOS checkpoint (baseline: NMOS DC 14.72 %, VTC
19.15 %). We tested whether a modest (30-epoch, low-LR) **tech-
specific fine-tune** on TSMC7 data would lift TSMC7 accuracy without
regressing the other TSMC techs.

## Environment

- **Branch:** `feat/bsimar-v4-tech-code`
- **Baseline commit:** `11f8999` (after E1 revert)
- **Harness:** `external_compact_models/bsimar/training/finetune.py :: finetune_v4`
- **Pretrained seed:** `v4_universal_nmos_best.phys.pt` (5.02 M params,
  TSMC-only universal, `phys-best` val variant)
- **Data:** `external_compact_models/bsimar/data/datasets/universal_nmos.npz`
- **Hardware:** A100-PCIE-40GB x 1, PyCMG-pycircuitsim conda env, CUDA

Script summary line: `Result: 4/8 PASS` (NMOS DC AR+DN FAIL, VTC AR+DN
FAIL — exactly the same set as baseline).

**Critical observation:** although the fine-tune achieved excellent
TSMC7 val-set accuracy (0.45 % NRMSE_phys, R²=0.9924), the verifier's
NMOS DC sweep NRMSE essentially did not change (14.72 → 14.74). The
Id-Vgs plot (`tests/verify_bsimar_v4_inverter_results/TSMC7/nmos_dc.png`)
shows the same family-wide under-prediction: BSIMAR saturates near
-500 µA at Vgs=750 mV whereas PyCMG saturates near -620 µA — identical
to the pre-FT curve. The fine-tune gained 0.45 % mean NRMSE on the
full TSMC7 bias distribution, but the specific operating points that
the verifier samples (Vds=VDD/2=0.375 V, Vbs=0 V, L=16 nm, NFIN=10)
apparently sit in a thin sliver of the distribution that does not
dominate the training loss.

## Step 3 — Regression sanity check on TSMC12

Expected: regression, since the fine-tune specialised the NMOS model
to TSMC7. Run:
`conda run -n pycircuitsim python tests/verify_bsimar_v4_inverter.py --tech tsmc12 --bsimar-prefix v4_ft_tsmc7`

Full log: `results/v5_e3_verify_tsmc12_regression.log`. Wall-clock ~20 min.

| Metric                  | Baseline | E3 (ft NMOS + univ PMOS) | Delta (pp) |
|-------------------------|----------|--------------------------|------------|
| **NMOS DC (AR)**        |  9.95    |  **7.59**                | **-2.36** ✅ |
| PMOS DC (AR)            | 13.72    | 13.72                    |  0.00      |
| **VTC (AR)**            |  4.10    |  **7.71**                | **+3.61** ❌ |
| **Transient (AR)**      |  6.78    |  **8.05**                | **+1.27**  |

(DN numbers left out — DirectNet checkpoint is unchanged; the AR rows
are the ones that reflect the fine-tune.)

NMOS DC on TSMC12 actually **improved** by 2.36 pp, which is
surprising (a TSMC7-specific fine-tune should hurt TSMC12 rather than
help it). The most likely explanation is that the universal seed was
mildly miscalibrated for TSMC12 NMOS and that the extra TSMC7 gradient
nudged a weight direction shared by both techs. The VTC, however,
regressed by 3.61 pp — above the 3 pp tolerance in the rubric — and
the transient regressed by 1.27 pp.

## Verdict

Rule (from the task spec):

> KEEP if BSIMAR TSMC7 NMOS DC drops by > 5 pp **AND** BSIMAR TSMC7
> VTC drops by > 5 pp **AND** no other-tech NMOS DC regresses > 3 pp.
> Else MARGINAL. Else REVERT.

Evaluating against the rule:

- **BSIMAR TSMC7 NMOS DC drop:** -0.02 pp (essentially zero; +0.02 pp
  if rounded). Needs < -5 pp. **FAIL**.
- **BSIMAR TSMC7 VTC drop:** -0.01 pp. Needs < -5 pp. **FAIL**.
- **Other-tech NMOS DC regression cap:** TSMC12 NMOS DC actually
  *improved* (-2.36 pp). **PASS**.

Since the first two hard gates both fail, and we see a measurable
(3.61 pp) regression in TSMC12 VTC, the verdict is **REVERT**.

### Recommendation for follow-up

The root cause of the flat DC accuracy is almost certainly **not** "the
model doesn't fit TSMC7 data" (val NRMSE = 0.45 %) but rather "the
verifier samples a specific corner (Vbs=0, L=16 nm, NFIN=10, Vds=VDD/2)
that is under-weighted in the training loss". Candidate next steps
(these are *hypotheses*, not a commitment):

1. Add a **Vbs=0, L=16 nm, NFIN=10 dense region** to the data
   generator and re-train (data fix, not model fix).
2. **Re-weight the loss** on the Vbs=0 slice during fine-tune (e.g.
   3× weight on rows with |Vbs| < 1 mV).
3. Investigate whether the post-FT model is still saturating near the
   `_vdd_estimate` rail — if the rail-restoring extrapolation is
   kicking in during the DC sweep at Vgs ≈ VDD, the Id curve will cap
   there regardless of any training improvement.

None of these were in scope for E3.
