## 2. Primary target — BSIMAR TSMC5 Transient

**Baseline:** 12.13 % → **E1:** 12.16 %  (**Δ = +0.03 pp**, flat).

The hypothesis that VT-bump would reduce BSIMAR TSMC5 transient **did
not hold**. The transient is essentially unchanged. The 37 % reduction
in small-|Vds| leakage factor that the plan predicted did not translate
into visible transient NRMSE improvement — likely because:

1. TSMC5's BSIMAR transient error at 12 % is not dominated by near-rail
   sub-threshold leakage but by mid-range switching dynamics
   (|Vds| in the 0.1–0.5 V range, where both `1−exp(−|Vds|/VT)` values
   are close to 1.0 and the VT bump has minimal effect).
2. The BSIMAR wrong-sign subthreshold diagnosis may still be the right
   story but requires a retrain (sign-consistency loss), not a runtime VT
   bump — the `f_id` gate cannot fix sign errors, only attenuate them.

## 4. E1 verdict

**Acceptance (plan §12):**
- If ANY cell regresses > 1 pp → **E1 REVERT**.
- Else if BSIMAR TSMC5 Transient drops > 1 pp AND all others within ± 1 pp → **E1 KEEP**.
- Else → **E1 NEUTRAL REVERT**.

**Result:**
- Worst regression 0.37 pp < 1 pp: **does not trigger REVERT**.
- BSIMAR TSMC5 Tran Δ = +0.03 pp (flat, not a drop > 1 pp):
  **does not trigger KEEP**.
- ∴ **E1 NEUTRAL REVERT.**

**Verdict: NEUTRAL → revert the 1-line change.**

The VT bump is safe (no cell regresses materially) but the primary
target showed no improvement. Keeping the bump would shift all DC cells
by ~0.2 pp in the wrong direction with no transient win to offset it.
Best to revert and move on to S3 (per-tech fine-tune) or S2 (tanh gate
retrain), which target the root cause (training sign/boundary, not
inference gating).
